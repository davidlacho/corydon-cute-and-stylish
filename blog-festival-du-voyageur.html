What is Festival du Voyageur?

Festival du Voyageur, affectionately known as "Festival" by locals, pays homage to the voyageurs‚Äîthe French-Canadian fur traders who played a crucial role in Canada's early history. What to See and Do

Snow Sculptures

One of the festival's most spectacular features is the international snow sculpture competition. Talented artists from around the world transform blocks of snow into intricate works of art, creating a winter wonderland that showcases incredible creativity and skill. These sculptures are illuminated at night, creating a magical atmosphere.

Historical Reenactments

Step back in time at Fort Gibraltar, where historical interpreters bring the fur-trading era to life. Watch demonstrations of traditional skills, learn about voyageur life, and experience what it was like to be part of Canada's early trading networks. The fort's authentic buildings and costumed interpreters create an immersive historical experience.

Music and Entertainment

Music is central to Festival du Voyageur, with stages featuring everything from traditional French-Canadian folk music to contemporary performances. You'll hear fiddles, accordions, and toe-tapping rhythms that will have you dancing despite the cold. The festival showcases both local talent and renowned artists from across Canada.

Family Activities

Families will find plenty to enjoy, including:

  • Horse-drawn sleigh rides through the festival grounds
  • Tobogganing hills for kids of all ages
  • Interactive workshops teaching traditional crafts
  • Storytelling sessions sharing voyageur tales
  • Children's activities and games
