Winnipeg bakeries and artisan bread in 2026

🥖 Winnipeg dining • ⏱️ 9 min read • 📅 May 7, 2026

Winnipeg supports both large wholesale bakeries and small rooms that mill flour or ferment dough over multiple days. Visitors often combine a bakery stop with coffee; our coffee guide lists specialty cafes near major shopping corridors.

Tall Grass Prairie Bread Company

Tall Grass Prairie Bread Co. runs a long-standing counter inside The Forks Market at 1 Forks Market Road. The Forks lists daily hours (typically 7:00 a.m. to 7:00 p.m., with annual closures on Remembrance Day and Christmas Day). The bakery mills organic heritage wheat and sells desem-style loaves, croissants, cinnamon buns, sandwiches, and soups.

Why go: Central tourist location, organic grain focus, and quick pairing with other Forks Market vendors.

Forgotten Flavours Artisan Bakery

Forgotten Flavours operates at 858 Corydon Avenue. Tourism Winnipeg and local press describe the bakery's wild-yeast sourdough program and multi-day fermentation. The shop sells bread, pastries, and dry goods; hours and the week's bake list belong on the bakery's social channels or phone line before you walk over from Crescentwood.

Why go: Walking distance from Corydon Avenue walks and a concentrated sourdough selection.

Gunn's Bakery

Gunn's Bakery on Sargent Avenue has supplied Winnipeg since 1937 with Eastern European breads, bagels, and pastries. It fills a different niche than sourdough-focused rooms: bulk buns, cakes, and weekday lunch traffic.

Why go: Historic family bakery, strong bagel and rye bread reputation, and a distinct west-end pilgrimage from the artisan circuit.

Neighbourhood franchises with full baking programs

COBS Bread and similar chains operate Manitoba franchises with daily production; Cory Common Bakery on Corydon lists opening hours through the national site. These shops suit predictable croissant or loaf pickups when artisan rooms sell out.

Sell-out reality: Small bakeries often post daily menus online. Saturday mornings at farmers' markets (see our farmers' market guide) move inventory quickly; arrive early if you need specific loaves.

How to build a bakery crawl

One reasonable day starts at Tall Grass inside The Forks, walks the river trails, then returns toward Corydon for Forgotten Flavours or a franchise pickup before heading home to Corydon Cute & Stylish. Add Gunn's when you want Eastern European items not duplicated elsewhere.
