The Winnipeg Jets: A Story of Resilience, Passion, and Hockey Heritage

The Winnipeg Jets represent more than just a hockey team‚Äîthey embody the resilience, passion, and unwavering spirit of a city that refused to let go of its hockey dreams. From their groundbreaking origins in the World Hockey Association to their triumphant return to the NHL, the Jets' story is one of determination, community support, and the deep love that Winnipeggers have for the game. The Birth of a Legend: The WHA Years (1972-1979)

When the World Hockey Association (WHA) was formed in 1972, Winnipeg was chosen as one of the founding cities. The original Jets quickly established themselves as a powerhouse, making a bold move that would change professional hockey forever: they signed NHL superstar Bobby Hull to a groundbreaking contract worth $1 million over 10 years, plus a $1 million signing bonus. This move not only legitimized the WHA but also made Hull the face of the Winnipeg franchise.

But the Jets' innovation didn't stop there. Under the leadership of coach Rudy Pilous and later Bobby Kromm, the team became pioneers in recruiting European talent. Swedish stars Anders Hedberg and Ulf Nilsson joined Hull to form the legendary "Hot Line," a trio that dominated the league and revolutionized how North American hockey was played. Their success demonstrated that European players could not only compete but excel at the highest levels of professional hockey.

The Jets' WHA tenure was nothing short of spectacular. They won three Avco World Trophies (the WHA's championship) in 1976, 1978, and 1979, and made five finals appearances during the league's seven seasons. This period established Winnipeg as a hockey city and created a passionate fanbase that would prove unshakeable in the decades to come.

Did You Know? The Jets' success in the WHA helped pave the way for the eventual merger with the NHL. Their dominance, combined with their innovative approach to player recruitment, demonstrated that the WHA was a legitimate competitor to the established NHL.

Entering the NHL: Challenges and Perseverance (1979-1996)

In 1979, the WHA merged with the NHL, and the Jets were among four teams that made the transition. However, the merger came with significant challenges. The NHL imposed restrictions that forced the Jets to give up many of their best players, including Hedberg and Nilsson, who were claimed by the New York Rangers. Despite these setbacks, the Jets entered the NHL with determination and a loyal fanbase.

Over the next 15 seasons, the Jets made 11 playoff appearances, but they struggled to advance deep into the postseason. They often found themselves competing against powerhouse teams like the Edmonton Oilers (led by Wayne Gretzky) and the Calgary Flames, making playoff success difficult to achieve. Despite these challenges, the team developed memorable players like Dale Hawerchuk, who became the face of the franchise during the 1980s.

However, financial difficulties and the economic realities of operating in a smaller market began to take their toll. The Canadian dollar's weakness, combined with rising player salaries and the challenges of Winnipeg's smaller market size, made it increasingly difficult for the team to remain competitive. In 1996, after years of financial struggles, the franchise relocated to Phoenix, Arizona, where they became the Coyotes.

The loss of the Jets was devastating for Winnipeg. For 15 years, the city that had embraced hockey with such passion was left without an NHL team, though the memory of the Jets and the hope for their return never faded.

The Return: True North and the Revival (2011-Present)

Hockey returned to Winnipeg in 2011, but the story of the Jets' revival actually began years earlier. True North Sports & Entertainment, led by Mark Chipman and David Thomson, had been working behind the scenes to bring the NHL back to Winnipeg. When the Atlanta Thrashers became available, True North seized the opportunity, purchasing the franchise and relocating it to Winnipeg.

The announcement that the Jets were returning sent shockwaves of joy through the city. Season tickets sold out in minutes, and the passion that had been dormant for 15 years erupted with incredible intensity. The team chose to honor the original Jets by reviving the name, logo, and colors, creating a powerful connection between the past and present.

Since their return, the Jets have experienced significant milestones. In the 2017-18 season, they reached the Western Conference Finals, their deepest playoff run since returning to the NHL. The team has developed a reputation for strong drafting and player development, with stars like Mark Scheifele, Kyle Connor, and Connor Hellebuyck becoming franchise cornerstones.

The 2024-25 season marked a historic achievement: the Jets finished with a franchise-best record of 56-22-4, earning the Presidents' Trophy for the league's best regular-season record. This accomplishment demonstrated how far the team had come since their return and solidified their status as one of the NHL's elite franchises.

Building a Winner: The Jets' success since their return has been built on strong drafting, patient development, and a commitment to building from within. This approach has created a sustainable model for success in the NHL's salary cap era.

The 2026 Heritage Classic: A Return to Outdoor Hockey

The NHL is bringing back the Heritage Classic next season, and Winnipeg will once again be at the center of hockey history. On October 25, 2026, the Winnipeg Jets will host the Montreal Canadiens in the 2026 Tim Hortons NHL Heritage Classic at Princess Auto Stadium, home of the Winnipeg Blue Bombers of the Canadian Football League.

This will be the eighth Heritage Classic game and the 46th NHL outdoor regular-season game overall. It marks the Jets' second time hosting the Heritage Classic at Princess Auto Stadium; their first hosting experience came on October 23, 2016, when they lost 3-0 to the Edmonton Oilers in front of 33,240 passionate fans.

For the Jets, this will be their third outdoor game, all in the Heritage Classic series. They hold a 1-1-0 record in outdoor games, having lost to the Oilers in 2016 before securing a memorable 2-1 overtime victory against the Calgary Flames at Mosaic Stadium in Regina, Saskatchewan, on October 26, 2019.

The Montreal Canadiens, one of hockey's most storied franchises, will be playing their first outdoor game since 2017 and their fifth overall (2-2-0). The Canadiens were part of the very first Heritage Classic on November 22, 2003, when they defeated the Oilers 4-3 at Commonwealth Stadium in Edmonton‚Äîthe first NHL regular-season outdoor game ever played.

Heritage Classic History: The Heritage Classic was introduced by the NHL on November 22, 2003, when the Canadiens defeated the Oilers 4-3 in the first NHL regular-season outdoor game at Commonwealth Stadium. This game proved that outdoor hockey could work at the professional level and launched a tradition that has become one of the NHL's most beloved events.

Tim Hortons and the Heritage Classic Partnership

Tim Hortons returns as the title sponsor of the NHL Heritage Classic, marking the seventh time the iconic Canadian brand has sponsored the event. As part of the announcement of the 2026 game, Tim Hortons and the NHL announced a new multiyear deal of their Canadian partnership that includes Tim Hortons maintaining its status as the Official Coffee and Coffee Based Beverages, Tea, Hot Chocolate, Breakfast and Baked Goods of the NHL in Canada.

This partnership extends beyond just the Heritage Classic. Tim Hortons supports the full hockey spectrum, from Timbits minor hockey to junior hockey, and the NHL's seven Canadian member clubs. The brand has enhanced the NHL fan experience through its partnership at other tentpole League events and programming, including NHL Street, the 2016 World Cup of Hockey, the 2024 Rogers NHL All-Star, and the 2025 4 Nations Face-Off.

For more than 10 years, Tim Hortons has engaged hockey fans coast to coast with one of the most successful and iconic sponsorship programs in the Canadian marketplace‚ÄîTim Hortons NHL Hockey Cards. The Jets' Outdoor Game History

The Jets have a special relationship with outdoor hockey. Their first outdoor game came in 2016 at Princess Auto Stadium, where they hosted the Edmonton Oilers. Though the Jets lost 3-0, the event was a massive success, drawing 33,240 fans and demonstrating Winnipeg's passion for hockey in all its forms.

Three years later, the Jets traveled to Regina, Saskatchewan, to face the Calgary Flames at Mosaic Stadium. In a thrilling game that went to overtime, the Jets emerged victorious with a 2-1 win, giving them their first outdoor game victory. This game showcased the Jets' ability to perform in the unique outdoor environment and added another memorable chapter to the team's history.

The 2026 Heritage Classic will be the Jets' third outdoor game, and hosting it at Princess Auto Stadium brings the story full circle. The venue that hosted their first outdoor game will now host what promises to be an even more spectacular event, with the Jets having established themselves as one of the NHL's premier franchises.
