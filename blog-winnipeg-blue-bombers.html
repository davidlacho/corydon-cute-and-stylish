A Rich History: From 1930 to Today

The Winnipeg Blue Bombers were established in 1930, making them one of the oldest and most storied franchises in the CFL. Over the course of nearly a century, the team has built a legacy of excellence that includes 12 Grey Cup championships, with their most recent victory coming in 2021. This championship was particularly meaningful, as it ended a 29-year drought and marked the team's return to the pinnacle of Canadian football.

The Blue Bombers' history is filled with legendary players, memorable games, and championship moments that have become part of Winnipeg's cultural fabric. From the early days of the Western Interprovincial Football Union (WIFU) to their current status as perennial contenders in the CFL's West Division, the Blue Bombers have consistently been one of the league's most competitive and respected teams.

Did You Know? The Blue Bombers' name is said to have originated from a 1935 newspaper headline that described the team's powerful play as "bombing" their opponents. The name stuck, and the team has been known as the Blue Bombers ever since, with their distinctive blue and gold colours becoming synonymous with Winnipeg football.

Recent Success: A Dynasty in the Making

Since 2019, the Winnipeg Blue Bombers have established themselves as one of the CFL's dominant teams, making five consecutive Grey Cup appearances‚Äîa remarkable achievement that demonstrates both consistency and excellence. This unprecedented run began with their 2019 Grey Cup victory, which ended a 29-year championship drought and sparked a new era of success for the franchise.

The 2019 Championship Season

The 2019 season was a turning point for the Blue Bombers. After finishing the regular season with an 11-7 record, the team embarked on a playoff run that culminated in a 33-12 victory over the Hamilton Tiger-Cats in the Grey Cup. This championship was particularly significant because it was the team's first since 1990, and it marked the beginning of a period of sustained excellence that has continued through the 2025 season.

Continued Excellence (2020-2025)

Following their 2019 championship, the Blue Bombers continued their winning ways. In 2021, they captured their second Grey Cup in three years, defeating the Hamilton Tiger-Cats once again. The team's success has been built on a foundation of strong coaching, excellent player development, and a commitment to building a winning culture.

The 2023 season saw the Blue Bombers finish with an impressive 14-4 record, securing first place in the West Division for the fourth consecutive year. They advanced to their fourth straight Grey Cup but were narrowly defeated by the Montreal Alouettes in a hard-fought game. The 2024 season followed a similar pattern, with the team finishing 11-7 and again winning the West Division, only to fall to the Toronto Argonauts in the Grey Cup.

In 2025, the Blue Bombers finished with a 10-8 record, placing fourth in the West Division but still qualifying for the playoffs for the ninth consecutive year. Despite being eliminated in the East Semi-Final by the Montreal Alouettes, the team's consistent playoff appearances demonstrate their status as one of the CFL's premier franchises.

Building a Winner: The Blue Bombers' recent success has been built on strong leadership from head coach Mike O'Shea and general manager Kyle Walters, who have created a culture of excellence that has made the team a perennial contender. Their commitment to organizational stability and player development has been key to the team's sustained success.

Princess Auto Stadium: The Home of the Blue Bombers

Princess Auto Stadium, located in Winnipeg's Polo Park area, is the home of the Winnipeg Blue Bombers and one of the premier football venues in Canada. The stadium, which opened in 2013, replaced the old Canad Inns Stadium and has become a state-of-the-art facility that provides an exceptional game-day experience for fans.

The stadium's design prioritizes fan experience, with excellent sightlines from every seat, modern amenities, and a capacity that allows for the electric atmosphere that has become synonymous with Blue Bombers games.
