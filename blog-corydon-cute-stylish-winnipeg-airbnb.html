Historic Crescentwood: Where Heritage Meets Modern Living

Crescentwood is one of Winnipeg's most distinguished neighborhoods, known for its tree-lined streets, beautiful architecture, and rich history. Originally developed in the early 1900s as an exclusive residential area for Winnipeg's elite, the neighborhood has maintained its charm while embracing modern urban living.

Walking through Crescentwood, you'll discover grand heritage homes, including the famous "Monster Mansion" at 10 Ruskin Row, alongside beautifully maintained mid-century properties like ours. The neighborhood's character and walkability make it a favorite among both locals and visitors.

Did You Know? Ruskin Row was named after the famous English art critic John Ruskin and was home to many of Winnipeg's most prominent citizens in the early 20th century, including lawyers, bankers, and even a Bank of Canada Governor. Learn more about the original residents of Ruskin Row.
