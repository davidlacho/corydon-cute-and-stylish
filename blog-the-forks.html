At the heart of Winnipeg, where the Red and Assiniboine Rivers meet, lies one of North America's most significant historic sites: The Forks. This isn't just a tourist attraction‚Äîit's a place where 6,000 years of human history converge, where Indigenous peoples, fur traders, settlers, and modern Winnipeggers have all gathered. If you're visiting our city, The Forks is an essential destination that tells the story of Winnipeg itself.

A Meeting Place for Millennia

The Forks has been a gathering place for over 6,000 years. Indigenous peoples recognized the strategic importance of this location, where two major rivers meet, making it an ideal spot for trade, ceremony, and community. Archaeological evidence shows that this site has been continuously used by various First Nations communities long before European settlement.

When European fur traders arrived in the 18th century, they too recognized the value of this location. The Forks became a crucial trading post, connecting the vast network of waterways that stretched across the continent. The Hudson's Bay Company and North West Company both established posts here, making it one of the most important commercial centers in Western Canada.

What to See and Do at The Forks

The Forks Market

The Forks Market is the vibrant heart of this historic site. Housed in a beautifully restored stable building, the market features over 50 vendors offering everything from fresh produce and artisanal foods to unique crafts and souvenirs. Whether you're looking for a quick snack, a full meal, or a special gift, you'll find it here.

Forks Market Tower

Climb the Forks Market Tower for panoramic views of the rivers, downtown Winnipeg, and the surrounding area. The tower offers a unique perspective on the city and helps you understand why this location has been so important throughout history.

Oodena Celebration Circle

This outdoor amphitheater is a beautiful space for events, performances, and gatherings. The name "Oodena" means "heart of the community" in Ojibwe, reflecting the site's significance to Indigenous peoples. Check the schedule for concerts, cultural events, and festivals throughout the year.

Johnston Terminal

This historic warehouse building now houses restaurants, shops, and the Manitoba Children's Museum. The building itself is a testament to Winnipeg's industrial past, beautifully preserved and repurposed for modern use.

River Walk

Take a stroll along the river walkways that connect The Forks to other parts of the city. The paths offer beautiful views of the rivers, downtown skyline, and are perfect for walking, jogging, or cycling. In winter, parts of the river become skating trails‚Äîa truly magical Winnipeg experience.

Seasonal Activities

The Forks transforms with the seasons, offering different experiences year-round:

  • Summer: Outdoor concerts, festivals, farmers' markets, and river activities
  • Fall: Beautiful foliage along the river, harvest markets, and cultural events
  • Winter: Skating trails on the river, warming huts designed by international architects, and winter festivals
  • Spring: The return of outdoor activities, cherry blossoms, and the awakening of the riverside

Dining at The Forks

The Forks is a food lover's paradise, with options ranging from casual market fare to fine dining. Sample local specialties, international cuisine, or grab a coffee and watch the world go by. Many restaurants offer patios with river views, perfect for enjoying Winnipeg's beautiful summer weather.

Pro Tip: Visit The Forks early in the morning to experience it at its most peaceful, or come in the evening when the area comes alive with activity. The market is open year-round, but hours vary by season, so check The Forks website ahead. Parking is available, but The Forks is also easily accessible by public transit or a pleasant walk from downtown.

Cultural Significance

The Forks represents more than just a historic site‚Äîit's a living symbol of Winnipeg's diverse cultural heritage. The site honors the Indigenous peoples who first gathered here, the fur traders who built the city's economy, and the modern communities that continue to make this place vibrant and meaningful.

Throughout the year, The Forks hosts cultural events, Indigenous ceremonies, and educational programs that help visitors understand the deep history and ongoing significance of this special place.

Getting to The Forks

From our Airbnb in Crescentwood, The Forks is easily accessible by car (about 10 minutes), public transit, or even by bike along the river paths. The site is located at the junction of the Red and Assiniboine Rivers, just south of downtown Winnipeg.

It's where you can feel the pulse of Winnipeg, understand its past, and experience its present. Whether you're interested in history, shopping, dining, or simply enjoying a beautiful riverside setting, The Forks offers something for everyone.

When you visit, take a moment to stand at the confluence of the two rivers and imagine the thousands of years of human activity that have taken place on this very spot. The Forks isn't just a destination‚Äîit's a connection to the deep history and vibrant culture that make Winnipeg the remarkable city it is today.
