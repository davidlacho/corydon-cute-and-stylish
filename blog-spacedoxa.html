spacedoxa at Graffiti Gallery: Winnipeg's Experimental Performance Home

🎭 Arts & Culture • ⏱️ 7 min read • 📅 March 27, 2026

If you are looking for a Winnipeg arts night that feels local, raw, and genuinely creative, keep your eye on spacedoxa (also styled as space)doxa). The project describes itself as a hub for live music, performance art, and experimental cinema, and it is connected to a long-running monthly series hosted at Graffiti Gallery in the Exchange-adjacent core at 109 Higgins Avenue.

Quick snapshot: spacedoxa currently lists 100+ posts on Instagram and is based at Graffiti Gallery, 109 Higgins Ave, Winnipeg MB R3B 0B5. Follow updates here: spacedoxa on Instagram.

What Makes spacedoxa Worth Seeking Out

Many cities have polished stages and ticketed touring shows. spacedoxa feels different: artist-led, process-driven, and often focused on work that does not fit neatly into mainstream categories. A single evening can move between sound experiments, movement-based performance, projected visuals, and boundary-pushing live sets. That openness is exactly why these events matter.

For visitors staying in Crescentwood near Corydon, this is also a chance to experience Winnipeg's independent arts ecosystem beyond the usual museum-and-theatre circuit. If your trip includes one "I had no idea this existed here" moment, this can be it.

The Venue: Graffiti Gallery and Graffiti Art Programming

spacedoxa's long-running monthly series is hosted at Graffiti Gallery, which is part of Graffiti Art Programming Inc., a Winnipeg not-for-profit youth arts organization. Their broader mission uses art for community development, social change, and individual growth.

That context matters. The gallery is not just a room for events; it is part of a wider community ecosystem that includes youth programming, workshops, artist mentorship, and public art. The organization describes each visit to Graffiti Gallery as unique because the walls are always evolving.

Good to know: Graffiti Gallery also supports programming like st.ART (free after-school and summer activities for children and youth), along with initiatives such as Studio 393. Seeing spacedoxa in this setting gives the series a real community foundation.

Visiting Details and Planning Tips

Location: 109 Higgins Ave, Winnipeg, Manitoba R3B 0B5.

According to Graffiti Art Programming information, gallery visits are generally by appointment only. Hours and booking details can change depending on exhibitions and programming cycles, so confirm on the official site before you go.

  • Check spacedoxa social updates first for date and lineup changes.
  • Verify venue access details directly with Graffiti Gallery in advance.
  • Plan transport ahead of time if you are visiting from Corydon/Crescentwood.
  • Arrive a bit early for first-time visits so you can settle into the space.

Why This Fits a Winnipeg Stay

At Stay Winnipeg, we often suggest a mix of iconic attractions and neighbourhood-level experiences. spacedoxa fits that second category perfectly: artist-run energy, experimental programming, and a venue tied to youth and community arts work in the city core.

If your travel style leans curious and culture-forward, add spacedoxa to your shortlist. It is one of those local spaces where Winnipeg's creative scene feels immediate and alive.
