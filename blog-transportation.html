Getting Around Winnipeg: Transportation Guide for Visitors

Winnipeg is a spread-out city, but getting around is straightforward with multiple transportation options. Here's everything you need to know about navigating the city.

Public Transit

Winnipeg Transit

The city's bus system covers most areas, including routes to major attractions. Fares are affordable, and you can pay with cash, tickets, or a reloadable Peggo card. The Transit app makes planning routes easy.

Key Routes for Visitors

  • Routes connecting downtown to The Forks
  • Buses serving Corydon Avenue and surrounding neighborhoods
  • Routes to Assiniboine Park and Zoo
  • Airport express service

Rideshare & Taxis

Uber and traditional taxi services operate throughout Winnipeg. Rideshare is convenient for getting to specific destinations, especially if you're traveling with luggage or in a group.

Driving & Car Rental

Winnipeg is a car-friendly city with generally easy parking compared to larger metropolitan areas. Major car rental companies operate at the airport and throughout the city. If you're staying in Corydon, street parking is typically available, though be mindful of parking restrictions.

Walking & Biking

Winnipeg is becoming increasingly walkable and bike-friendly, especially in neighborhoods like Corydon, The Exchange District, and The Forks. The city has been expanding its bike lane network, and bike share programs are available in some areas.

Getting to Winnipeg

By Air

Winnipeg James Armstrong Richardson International Airport (YWG) is located about 20 minutes from downtown. The airport is well-connected with flights to major Canadian cities and some U.S. destinations.

By Car

Winnipeg is accessible via major highways. The Trans-Canada Highway runs through the city, making it easy to reach from other parts of Canada.

By Bus

Greyhound and other bus services connect Winnipeg to other cities in Manitoba and beyond.

By Train

VIA Rail provides passenger rail service to Winnipeg, connecting the city to other major Canadian destinations.

Pro Tip: If you're staying in the Corydon area, many attractions and restaurants are within walking distance. For longer trips, public transit or rideshare are convenient options. Consider the weather when planning - Winnipeg winters can be very cold, so you may prefer driving or transit during those months.

Parking Tips

  • Street parking is metered in many areas - check signs for restrictions
  • Some areas have free parking on evenings and weekends
  • Major attractions typically have parking lots or nearby parking
  • Residential areas like Corydon may have permit parking - check signs
